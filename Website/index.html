SmokeMon

Members: Stefany Cruz, Lenin Estrada, Christine Chen

ABSTRACT - Smoking is a major health concern around the world. However, due to the likely biases imposed on self-reports and self-monitoring, it is difficult to monitor smoking behaviors and associated characteristics of smoking. We propose SmokeMon, a chest-worn device that uses a thermal IR sensor array to detect heat generated from smoking and use the data to study the puff generated by smoking. Using the device, we will be able to detect smoking events and extract smoking topography, which is how a person smokes a cigarette including measures of the number of puffs and puff volume, duration and velocity. With SmokeMon, we monitor smoking events more closely and more accurately.
